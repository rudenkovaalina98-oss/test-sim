You've completed your practice and developed the core skills needed to guide meaningful conversations. This simulation is your opportunity to bring everything together in one final consultation.

Rather than introducing new challenges, this assessment measures your overall readiness to communicate with confidence, empathy, and professionalism in a realistic client interaction.
You'll have a session with the client Rachel. Before the chat begins, you'll see a short summary about her. Take a quiet moment with it. Let yourself understand who she is, what she might be feeling, and how you'd like to meet her. There's no rush in those first seconds — just awareness and intention.

The live chat will last exactly 3 minutes. Within that time, simply focus on being present, attentive, and clear. After the timer ends, you'll have one final message to close the session thoughtfully — a gentle way to leave warmth and depth behind you. After this write END SESSION to the bot and get your personal feedback. Copy your score and feedback before the next step and save your results.
  • 🔮 Answer Sufficiency — Deliver a clear, specific, and personalized reading that directly addresses the client's question with meaningful insights and relevant guidance.
  • 💜 Empathy — Recognize and validate the client's emotions while maintaining a warm, supportive, and human tone throughout the conversation.
  • 🔗 Engagement — Guide the conversation by asking thoughtful follow-up questions, exploring meaningful angles, and checking whether your insights resonate with the client.
  • ✨ Intrigue at the End — Conclude the consultation with a memorable closing that leaves the client feeling supported while naturally encouraging them to continue their journey.
  • 🎙️ Tone of Voice — Communicate in a natural, conversational, and authentic way. Avoid robotic, repetitive, or overly formal language, and make every response feel personal and engaging.
This assessment happens only once because it reflects your overall readiness at the end of the Trial journey. Trust your preparation, stay present, and approach the conversation as you would with a real client.
